Project Description

This program trains volunteers to become certified mental health first aid responders, capable of providing initial support to people experiencing mental health crises until appropriate professional help can be engaged.

Objectives

  • Train community members in mental health first aid techniques
  • Create a network of first responders for mental health emergencies
  • Reduce stigma around mental health issues through education

Target Audience

Adults interested in mental health advocacy, community leaders, teachers, and healthcare workers

Volunteer Roles

  • Participate in certification training courses
  • Staff crisis helplines after certification
  • Assist with community workshops and awareness sessions
  • Provide support at community events and gatherings

Impact

Train 200 new mental health first responders annually and provide critical early intervention to those in crisis
